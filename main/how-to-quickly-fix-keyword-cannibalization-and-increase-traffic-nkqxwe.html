How To Quickly Fix Keyword Cannibalization and Increase Traffic

Identify keyword cannibalization issues

 Look for keyword duplicates, and analyze your content to identify any duplicate or similar content that could be cannibalizing your keywords.

Optimize your existing content

Take the time to review and optimize any content that is competing against itself for the same keywords.

Create content silos

                                  

 Group related topics into silos to prevent keyword cannibalization from occurring.

Use different keyword variations

Include different keyword variations in your content to ensure that no two pages are competing for the same keyword.

Analyze and adjust your internal linking structure

 Ensure that you are linking to the most relevant pages, and that the link structure is optimized for SEO.

Monitor changes in keyword rankings

Regularly monitor your keyword rankings to identify any sudden drops, which could be due to keyword cannibalization.
Focus on long-tail keywords
 Long-tail keywords tend to have fewer competitors, making them easier to rank for and more likely to bring in organic traffic.

Monitor your competitors

Regularly monitor your competitors to identify any changes they have made that could have an effect on your search rankings.
